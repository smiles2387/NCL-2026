## CTF Highlights & Reflections

### What I'm Most Proud Of

**Scoring 4,785 out of 5,360 in the Gymnasium** — the largest and most comprehensive game format — is my standout achievement. The Gymnasium covered all categories at depth, and maintaining consistent performance across every domain required both breadth of knowledge and sustained focus.

**The Individual Game (2,495 / 3,000)** tested how well I could perform independently under competition conditions, with no teammates to lean on. Finishing at 83.2% reinforced my confidence in working through challenges solo.

**Consistent performance across categories** like Log Analysis, Network Traffic Analysis, and Cryptography — areas I approached methodically and with strong fundamentals — anchored my overall scores across all four games.

### Areas I'm Continuing to Develop

The categories where I left the most points on the table — Enumeration & Exploitation and Web Application Exploitation — showed up consistently across all game formats. I'm actively working on these through hands-on labs to close that gap.

The Practice Game was where I identified those weak spots early, which let me adjust my approach heading into the Individual and Team games.

### Key Takeaways

The NCL gave me structured exposure to the full breadth of cybersecurity disciplines in a competitive, time-pressured environment. It confirmed that I enjoy the analytical, investigative side of security — log analysis, forensics, and network traffic — while motivating me to sharpen my offensive skills to become a more well-rounded practitioner.

More than the scores, the competition taught me *how to learn under pressure* — to read error messages carefully, to revisit assumptions, and to keep going when a challenge doesn't yield immediately.
